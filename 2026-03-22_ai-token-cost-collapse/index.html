The 99.7% Collapse: Cost of AI per Million Tokens, 2022–2026
▼ −99.7%
Input cost in USD per million tokens, selected frontier models — logarithmic scale
In November 2022, running a frontier AI model cost roughly $20 per million tokens. By February 2026, equivalent capability costs $0.05 — a 400-fold decline in under four years. The collapse was not gradual: it arrived in lurches. OpenAI's GPT-4 briefly spiked costs to $30 at launch, then efficiency gains and competition drove them down. The January 2025 DeepSeek shock — open-source models at a fraction of incumbent prices — forced every major provider to accelerate cuts within weeks. Yet total enterprise AI bills are rising sharply: API spending grew from $500 million in 2023 to $8.4 billion by mid-2025. Cheaper tokens have not reduced demand. They have detonated it.
