TSMC's revenue has more than quintupled since 2013, driven by the AI chip boom
Annual net revenue of Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company, US$bn
TSMC is the exclusive manufacturer of the world's most advanced chips, making it perhaps the single most strategically important company in the global economy. Its revenue trajectory maps almost perfectly onto the AI investment supercycle: after a brief 4% dip in 2023 caused by a consumer-electronics glut, revenues surged 25% in 2024 and a further 32% in 2025, reaching a record $116bn. In February 2026, TSMC's market capitalisation crossed $2 trillion for the first time. Whether AI infrastructure spending can sustain this growth — or whether a second inventory correction looms — is the defining question for the global chip industry.
Source: TSMC Annual Reports, SEC Form 20-F filings, 2013–2025  |  @enriqui
